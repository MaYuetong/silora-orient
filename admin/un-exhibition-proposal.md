## The Craft — What is Chānhuā? · 缠花是什么？

Chānhuā (缠花) is a traditional Chinese craft in which colourful silk threads are wound layer by layer around frames of copper wire and stiff paper, creating vivid three-dimensional flowers, birds, and jewellery. Origins: Northern Song Dynasty (960–1127 AD). Status: Chinese Intangible Cultural Heritage (非物质文化遗产). Two regional schools: Yingshan (英山, Hubei) in the north; Minnan (闽南, Fujian) in the south.

**The Four Defining Qualities · 四大特色:**

| Character | Meaning |
|-----------|---------|
| 小 Small | Compact, wearable, intimate in scale |
| 巧 Clever | Ingenious design — every thread deliberate |
| 精 Refined | Meticulous craftsmanship, hours of skilled handwork |
| 活 Flexible | Endless forms — flowers, birds, landscapes, jewellery |

## Studio Values · 价值观

### I. Sustainability 可持续
All silk threads, paper, and wood-pulp card are 100% biodegradable. Jewel accents are responsibly sourced. Packaging is fully compostable from first stitch to final box.

### II. Social Equity 社会公平
Over five years, actively partnered with unemployed women in Sichuan Province, bringing them into the design and production process — giving their craft skills a platform and a livelihood.

### III. Personal Connection 个人连结
Each piece is fully customised, linking the wearer to their own story. Using orchid (兰花) as the signature motif, designs reflect the client's personal narrative, deepening their bond with living heritage.

## Featured Collections · 代表系列

All works are named from classical Chinese poetry — each piece a wearable poem.

### I. Phalaenopsis Jewellery Series 蝴蝶兰系列
Seven colourways each evoking a distinct sensory world: Honey Peach Oolong, Starry Moon Night, Frozen Wonderland, Gilded Dreams, and more. Available as earrings (耳钉), rings (戒指), pendants (挂坠). Clip-on (耳夹) available.

### II. Crystal Curtain 却下水晶帘 · Hairpin & Brooch Set
Inspired by Li Bai: 「却下水晶帘，玲珑望秋月。」 — a luminous moon disc, lavender flowers, crystal chain tassels (水晶流苏). Hairpin set + companion brooch.

### III. Osmanthus Series 月桂流金 · 四时之景
★ Featured on CCTV Spring Festival Gala 2026 ★  
Gold osmanthus clusters, deep olive-green leaves. Three forms: Lattice Brooches (花窗胸针), Hairpin (发簪), Hair Crown (发冠).

### IV. Lily in Moonlight 月下逢 · 四时之景
Inspired by Du Fu: 「若非群玉山头见，会向瑶台月下逢。」 — white-to-blue ombré petals, amber and jade beads, flowing luminosity (流光感). Full set: hairpin, brooches, ring.

### V. Lotus in the Wind 一一风荷举 · 四时之景
Inspired by Zhou Bangyan: 「水面清圆，一一风荷举。」 — pale green and blush-white petals, freshwater pearls, jade beads. Full set: hairpin, brooches, ring, earrings.

## Exhibition Proposal · 展览提案

### Why Chānhuā at the UN? · 为何将缠花带入联合国？

Chānhuā is a craft so intricate it must be seen up close — creating genuine, intimate dialogue between visitor and living tradition. Every piece carries a classical verse, giving international visitors both a visual experience and a literary entry into Chinese culture. Ma Yuetong currently works at UN HQ New York, personally bridging the craft with the institution's cultural mission.

### Exhibition Format · 展览形式

- Table-top display, approximately 0.5 m² (50 cm × 100 cm)
- Self-contained — no installation required, setup under 30 minutes
- Bilingual display cards (English / Chinese) for every work
- Studio introduction panel and artist biography
- Optional: live demonstration of silk-winding technique

### Availability Note · 参展说明

**UN Chinese Language Day (April 2026, New York):** Curated selection of pieces currently in New York — limited but refined display.

**Mid-Autumn Festival (future):** Full collection can be arranged including Osmanthus Series (CCTV Spring Gala 2026) and additional seasonal works — richer, more comprehensive exhibition possible.
